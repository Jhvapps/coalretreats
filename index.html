Our Story

Two friends.
One mountain.

Jack and Daniel met seventeen years ago in a ski season and have been lifelong friends ever since. Same mountains, same long dinners, same love of good food, the outdoors, and filling a week with more than most people fit into a month.

The building has a story of its own. James found it as an 1880s village post office and rebuilt it by hand into a catered chalet. His craftsmanship is in every beam, every piece of joinery, every detail that makes the place feel like a proper mountain home rather than a rental. Daniel and Zoe ran the catered chalet for him eight years ago, cooking and hosting, and the valley got under their skin.

Last year, Jack and Daniel took it on themselves. James is still close to the place and to us, and always will be. Now we run it the way we always wanted to be looked after: proper food, proper hosting, proper time in the mountains, and we want you to experience it too.

Food & Drink

Cook for yourselves.
Or let us look after you.

Two ways to eat at L’Ancienne Poste. Self-cater in a kitchen built for a group, or hand it over to our team of fully trained chefs and sit down to dinner. Whichever you choose, you eat really well.

When we cook, we celebrate what the Pyrenées does best, garbure, local duck, black pig, Tarbais beans, mountain cheese, but we take our influence from all over the world. The wine comes from local suppliers we know and trust. Restaurants and the nearest bakery aren’t on the doorstep, but they aren’t far.

Getting Here

Easy to reach.
Hard to leave.

Avajan sits at the heart of the Louron Valley, one of the Hautes-Pyrénées’ best-kept secrets. Toulouse airport is 1h 20m by road; Tarbes is closer still. You can also take the train via Lannemezan.

The valley itself is quiet and non-commercial, there’s no resort economy at the door. Just a working mountain village, extraordinary scenery, and everything you need within a short drive.
